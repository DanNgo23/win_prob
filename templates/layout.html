About The Model

Trained on 10 years of NFL play-by-play data from 2009-18, the prediction model uses data on time remaining, down, yards to go, field position, score, homefield advantage, and difference in team strength to calculate the win probability (WP) of a team in any specific game situation, during a game. When watching a game, feel free to use this web app to fill in the parameter values and click "Predict" to get the in-game WP.

About Win Probability

Any game situation can tell us the probability of that team going on to win the game. The model calculates the WP from the perspective of the team with the ball (the offense). To get the WP from the perspective of the defense, we take 1 - WP of the offense. The model implements logistic regression. It is a generalized linear model that uses weights for each predictor variable to estimate a probability between 0 and 1.

About Point Spreads

Point spreads are used as a proxy for estimated margin of victory/loss. It states "team A (the favorite) is better than team B (the underdog) by n points". We use them so every game does not open with 50/50 WP. We need to account for the strength of both teams throughout the game. Instead of using a rating for team A and a rating for team B, the estimated margin of victory has team strengths priced in with one number.
